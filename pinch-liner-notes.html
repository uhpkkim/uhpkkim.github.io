DEEP WIZARD THEORY ANALYSIS JUST FOR THE REAL FREAKS:
In the interest of transparency in an artform/culture that seems to love being cryptic and secretive, here is a full analysis of this track detailing my entire thought process. It's pretty dense. Just... feel free to hit me up if you wanna talk more about this nonsense. It's cool if you don't understand what's going on here - it's some deep nerd garbage for fucked up nerds. I hope it can at least inspire you to push your own songwriting.

Key/scale: the beginning is just in Ab dorian. But in the second half, the song goes through a cycle of chords. For each chord, the melody and bass dance around a different modality/scale vibe using each chord as a new tonal center. This is directly influenced by jazz chord-scale theory techniques, but used compositionally instead of improvisationally. The mode changes happen fluidly - often before the chord they're connected to shows up. Building the chord progression was informed by the modes being used, and visa versa. There were several layers of logic going on that I'm not going to go into here.

The chords / modes are:
Abm (dorian) B (lydian) Gb (lydian) Ab (mixolydian)
Ebm7 (dorian) Bbm (phrygian b4) Bm (harmonic major) Db (mixolydian).

At least, that’s how I was thinking about it when I wrote it. You could also more easily say that it changes key a few times: Ab dorian (Gb major), Ab mixolydian (Db major), Bb phrygian b4 (???). Or something like that. Listen. I got deep in it. After a certain point, I entered a trance and control was no longer in my hands and just had to let it happen.

Meter/time signature: The intro is in 4/4, then breaks down to a 6/4 groove, followed by a back-and-forth call and response between the established 6/4 groove guitar+banjo lines and the established 4/4 horn lines. Eventually it all melds back together into a stable 6/4 (where the horns and strings are joined together) except for the cheeky extra 2 beats at the end. This is just how I was thinking of it, you could probably interpret the meter a few different ways here. How did the time limit effect your songwriting?
This song has existed for a long time, maybe 20 years, and I've always thought of it as a fragment that needed to be fleshed out. In the summer of 2021 I had also made some field recordings of the Brood X cicadas, which I just adored. quit my job 

Any other thoughts / stories / insights about your song or the compilation?
I was once a prolific mix tape maker, and I was obsessive about filling all available space on the cassette. I mostly dubbed from my own cassette collection on to TDK D-90s, which usually included about 46 minutes of recording time on each side, and I kept a mental catalog of all the songs in my own library that ran just a minute or two — so lots of people got to hear the English Beat's "Click Click" and Meryn Cadell's "Being In Love." This compilation would have been a treasure trove for me in 1990.
